Research Agenda 2026–2031

A structured five-year roadmap for advancing structural systems intelligence, infrastructure resilience modeling, and AI-assisted analytical engineering.

Strategic Orientation

The five-year research agenda defines the phased development of analytical frameworks and structured research outputs aligned with institutional-level infrastructure challenges.

Phase I (2026–2027) – Foundation & Model Development

  • Refinement of AI-assisted seismic modeling frameworks
  • Development of probabilistic infrastructure interdependency models
  • Preparation of structured working papers for academic submission
  • Validation of nonlinear structural simulation workflows

Phase II (2027–2028) – System Integration & Publication

  • Integration of multi-layer infrastructure resilience models
  • Submission of peer-reviewed journal manuscripts
  • Expansion of cascading risk assessment methodologies
  • Cross-domain infrastructure scenario modeling

Phase III (2028–2031) – Strategic Deployment & Scaling

  • Scalable infrastructure intelligence framework development
  • National-scale analytical modeling applications
  • AI-enhanced decision-support system architecture
  • Institutional advisory-level research deployment

Research Governance Alignment

All agenda phases operate under structured governance principles, including methodological transparency, reproducibility, and institutional neutrality.

Publication Pathway

Research outputs developed under this agenda are intended for submission to peer-reviewed structural engineering and infrastructure systems journals, subject to formal acceptance processes.
