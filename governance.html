Governance Charter

Institutional governance framework of Sali Labs defining leadership structure, advisory development, and strategic authority principles.

Version 1.0

Effective Date: 2026

1. Institutional Identity

Sali Labs operates as an independent strategic research initiative focused on structural engineering intelligence, infrastructure resilience systems, and AI-assisted analytical frameworks.

2. Foundational Leadership

Sali Labs is founded and strategically directed by:

ABDOLSALEKH AGHATABAY
(Professionally known as Saleh Aghatabai)
Founder & Strategic Director

The Founder retains responsibility for strategic direction, research framework approval, institutional positioning, and external representation.

3. Advisory Framework

Sali Labs maintains an evolving Advisory Network to strengthen scientific rigor, institutional credibility, and cross-disciplinary analytical development.

Advisory participation may be formalized progressively as institutional development advances. The Advisory framework does not supersede the Founder’s strategic authority unless formally amended.

4. Decision Authority

Strategic and institutional decisions are made under the authority of the Founder & Strategic Director. Structured collaborations may be established through formal agreements when applicable.

5. Ethical & Analytical Principles

Sali Labs operates under principles of:

  • Strategic neutrality
  • Analytical independence
  • Evidence-based engineering logic
  • Long-horizon infrastructure thinking
  • Institutional transparency

6. Institutional Development Status

Sali Labs is currently under development toward formal legal registration as a structured research and strategic advisory entity. Until such registration is completed, the initiative functions as an independent analytical platform.

7. Amendment Clause

This Governance Charter may be amended under the authority of the Founder & Strategic Director. Future revisions shall include updated version numbers and effective dates.

8. Effective Authority

This Governance Charter Version 1.0 becomes effective as of 2026.
