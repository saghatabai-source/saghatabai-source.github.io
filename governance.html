Research Governance & Methodological Framework

This document outlines the governance principles and methodological standards guiding the research activities of Sali Labs as an independent structural engineering research initiative.

Research Governance Charter

Sali Labs operates under a structured research governance model designed to ensure analytical integrity, methodological transparency, and intellectual independence. All research activities are conducted in accordance with established engineering ethics, reproducibility standards, and rigorous validation procedures.

Research initiatives are formulated through hypothesis-driven inquiry, computational modeling, probabilistic assessment, and systematic validation cycles. Strategic applicability is evaluated through scenario-based analysis and long-horizon infrastructure assessment frameworks.

Sali Labs maintains full independence in research direction, topic selection, and analytical development unless formal collaborative agreements are explicitly stated.

Methodology Statement

The methodological architecture integrates advanced computational simulation, nonlinear structural modeling, probabilistic risk quantification, and infrastructure resilience analytics.

Research outputs follow a structured workflow:

  • Problem formulation and hypothesis definition
  • Computational model development
  • Validation and uncertainty quantification
  • Sensitivity analysis and performance assessment
  • Strategic systems integration

Emphasis is placed on reproducibility, methodological clarity, transparent assumptions, and scalable analytical design.

Where applicable, research findings are structured for academic dissemination, peer-reviewed publication, and institutional-level strategic consideration.
