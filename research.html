Independent Structural Engineering Research

Sali Labs is an independent structural engineering research initiative focused on advancing computational modeling, infrastructure resilience analysis, and AI-assisted structural systems. The research program is designed to combine analytical rigor with long-horizon strategic infrastructure thinking and applied engineering insight.

Research Identity

Sali Labs advances computational structural modeling, probabilistic infrastructure evaluation, and AI-assisted structural intelligence systems. The objective is to bridge analytical rigor with large-scale strategic infrastructure transformation.

Research Governance Model

The research framework follows a structured cycle including hypothesis formulation, computational modeling, validation, uncertainty quantification, and strategic applicability assessment. Emphasis is placed on methodological transparency, reproducibility, and analytical integrity.

Primary Research Domains

Seismic & Nonlinear Structural Systems

Performance-based seismic engineering, nonlinear finite element modeling, collapse mechanism analysis, and extreme-event structural response simulation.

Infrastructure Resilience Engineering

Multi-layer infrastructure coordination across transport, utilities, and energy networks using probabilistic risk assessment methodologies.

AI-Assisted Structural Intelligence

Integration of machine learning techniques with structural evaluation models to enhance predictive performance and system-level optimization.

Methodological Architecture

The methodological architecture integrates computational simulation, probabilistic modeling, system dynamics analysis, and scenario-based infrastructure forecasting. Research outputs are structured for academic dissemination and institutional-level strategic application.

Five-Year Research Agenda (2026–2031)

A structured five-year roadmap defines the progressive development of AI-assisted structural systems, infrastructure resilience modeling, and large-scale engineering intelligence frameworks. Peer-reviewed journal submissions are planned within this research horizon.
