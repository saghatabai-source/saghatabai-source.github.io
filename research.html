Research Framework

Sali Labs operates as an independent structural intelligence research initiative, integrating advanced engineering analytics with long-horizon infrastructure strategy.

Research Identity

Sali Labs is an independent structural research initiative dedicated to the development of advanced computational modeling systems, probabilistic infrastructure evaluation, and AI-assisted structural intelligence frameworks. The objective is to bridge analytical rigor with large-scale strategic infrastructure transformation models.



Research Governance Model

The research structure follows a formalized governance cycle including: hypothesis formulation, computational modeling, multi-stage validation, risk quantification, and strategic applicability assessment. Emphasis is placed on methodological transparency, reproducibility, and analytical integrity.



Primary Research Domains

Seismic & Nonlinear Structural Systems

Performance-based seismic engineering, nonlinear finite element modeling, collapse mechanism analysis, and extreme-event structural behavior simulation.

Infrastructure Resilience Engineering

Multi-layer infrastructure coordination across transport, utilities, and energy networks using probabilistic risk assessment frameworks.

AI-Assisted Structural Intelligence

Integration of machine learning methodologies with structural evaluation systems to enhance predictive modeling and system optimization.



Methodological Architecture

The methodological framework integrates computational simulation, probabilistic modeling, system dynamics analysis, and scenario-based infrastructure forecasting. Research outputs are structured for both academic dissemination and institutional-level strategic application.



Five-Year Research Agenda (2026–2031)

A structured five-year roadmap defines the progressive development of AI-assisted structural systems, infrastructure resilience modeling, and large-scale engineering intelligence frameworks. Targeted peer-reviewed journal submissions are scheduled within this research horizon.
